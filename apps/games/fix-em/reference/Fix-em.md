## A1:


### Passage 1: My Daily Routine


> "Every day, I gets up at 7:00 AM. I eats breakfast with my family. My brother like milk, but I prefers coffee. After breakfast, we goes to school by bus. The school are big and fun."


1. **Error:** `I gets up` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I get up` (Incorrect third-person -s on a first-person pronoun).
2. **Error:** `I eats` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I eat` (Same subject-verb agreement issue).
3. **Error:** `My brother like` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `My brother likes` (Missing the third-person -s for "he").
4. **Error:** `I prefers` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I prefer` (Incorrect -s).
5. **Error:** `we goes` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `we go` (Incorrect third-person plural form).
6. **Error:** `The school are` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `The school is` (The school is singular).


### Passage 2: At the Supermarket


> "Today, Sarah and John is at the supermarket. They wants to buy some fruits. Sarah need three apple and a banana. John look for some bread. They loves shopping together because it is easy."


1. **Error:** `Sarah and John is` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Sarah and John are` (Plural subject needs "are").
2. **Error:** `They wants` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `They want` (Incorrect third-person plural form).
3. **Error:** `Sarah need` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Sarah needs` (Missing third-person -s).
4. **Error:** `three apple` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `three apples` (Missing plural -s after a number plural).
5. **Error:** `John look for` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `John looks for` (Missing third-person -s).
6. **Error:** `They loves` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `They love` (Incorrect -s).


### Passage 3: My Favorite Pet


> "I have a dog. He name is Max. Max are two years old. He have beautiful brown hair and a short tail. Max like to run in the park on weekends. I love he very much."


1. **Error:** `He name` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `His name` (Using a subject pronoun instead of a possessive adjective).
2. **Error:** `Max are` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Max is` (Max is singular).
3. **Error:** `He have` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `He has` (Irregular third-person singular form of "to have").
4. **Error:** `Max like` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Max likes` (Missing third-person -s).
5. **Error:** `I love he` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I love him` (Using a subject pronoun instead of an object pronoun).

### Passage 1: Last Weekend


> "Last Saturday, I go to the beach with my friends. The weather was very hot, so we swimmed in the sea for hours. At lunch, we eated delicious sandwiches. In evening, we watched the sunset. It was more better than staying at home."


1. **Error:** `I go to the beach` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I went to the beach` (Past tense required for "Last Saturday").
2. **Error:** `we swimmed` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `we swam` (Incorrect regular past tense for the irregular verb "swim").
3. **Error:** `we eated` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `we ate` (Incorrect regular past tense for the irregular verb "eat").
4. **Error:** `In evening` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `In the evening` (Missing the definite article in the time phrase).
5. **Error:** `more better` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `better` (Double comparative; "better" is already the comparative form of "good").


### Passage 2: Planning a Trip


> "Next month, my family is going to visit London. We are exciting because we never went there before. My mother wants to see the Big Ben, but my father don't like crowded places. I think London is beautifuler than my hometown, so I can't wait."


1. **Error:** `We are exciting` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `We are excited` (-ed vs -ing adjective; the family *feels* excited, the trip itself is exciting).
2. **Error:** `my father don't like` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `my father doesn't like` (Third-person singular negative requires "doesn't").
3. **Error:** `the Big Ben` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Big Ben` (Do not use "the" before specific names of famous monuments/buildings like Big Ben).
4. **Error:** `beautifuler` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `more beautiful` (Long adjectives take "more" instead of "-er" for comparatives).


### Passage 3: My Cooking Hobby


> "I love cooking on my kitchen. I can to cook many different dishes, like pasta and pizza. Yesterday, I tried a new recipe, but I made it too quickly and it tasted badly. My brother said I should practicing more if I want to be a chef."


1. **Error:** `on my kitchen` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `in my kitchen` (Incorrect preposition of place).
2. **Error:** `I can to cook` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I can cook` (Modal verbs like "can" are followed by the bare infinitive, without "to").
3. **Error:** `it tasted badly` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `it tasted bad` (Verbs of sense like taste, smell, and look take adjectives, not adverbs, to describe the subject).
4. **Error:** `should practicing` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `should practice` (The modal verb "should" must be followed by the base form of the verb).

### Passage 1: The New Job


> "I have started my new job three weeks ago. I work as an assistant in a big company who designs software. I am very interested on this field, but the work is hard. If I will work overtime this week, my manager will give me a bonus next Monday."


1. **Error:** `I have started my new job three weeks ago.` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I started...` (The present perfect cannot be used with a specific past time marker like "three weeks ago"; use Past Simple).
2. **Error:** `a big company who designs` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `a big company which/that designs` (Relative pronoun "who" is only for people; companies take "which" or "that").
3. **Error:** `interested on` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `interested in` (Incorrect dependent preposition; the correct collocation is always "interested in").
4. **Error:** `If I will work overtime...` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `If I work overtime...` (First Conditional rule: the "if" clause uses the present simple, while the result clause uses "will").


### Passage 2: A Holiday in Rome


> "I have lived in this city since five years, but I never visited Italy until last summer. While I walked down a narrow street in Rome, I suddenly saw a beautiful hidden fountain. Many photos were took by me because the water looked so clear."


1. **Error:** `since five years` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `for five years` ("Since" is used for a specific starting point in time; "for" is used for a duration/length of time).
2. **Error:** `While I walked` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `While I was walking` (Past Continuous is needed here to show an ongoing action that was interrupted by a sudden event).
3. **Error:** `were took` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `were taken` (In the passive voice, the past participle "taken" must be used after the verb "to be," not the past simple "took").


### Passage 3: Learning English


> "Learning a new language is difficult, but it depends of how much you practice. I have been studying English for two years, and my speaking skills became much better. If you don't practice every day, you forgot new vocabulary quickly."


1. **Error:** `depends of` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `depends on` (Incorrect dependent preposition; the verb "depend" always pairs with "on").
2. **Error:** `my speaking skills became much better` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `have become much better` (Because the action started in the past and relates to the present—indicated by "for two years"—the Present Perfect is needed).
3. **Error:** `you forgot new vocabulary` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `you forget...` (This is a Zero Conditional stating a general truth/fact. Both clauses must be in the Present Simple).

### Passage 1: Changing Careers


> "By the time Mark turned thirty, he decided to change his career. He used to working as an accountant, but he hated sitting at a desk all day. If he had more free time, he would learn how to code, but his current job keeps him too busy. His friend suggested him to take an online course instead."


1. **Error:** `By the time Mark turned thirty, he decided...` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `he had decided...` (Past Perfect is required here because the decision happened *before* the specific past time marker of turning thirty).
2. **Error:** `He used to working` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `He used to work` ("Used to" for past habits/states is followed by the infinitive. "Be/Get used to" takes a gerund, but that doesn't fit the context of a past job).
3. **Error:** `suggested him to take` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `suggested that he take` or `suggested taking` (The verb "suggest" cannot be followed by an object pronoun + to-infinitive).


### Passage 2: The Interview


> "Yesterday, Sarah went to an interview for a graphic designer position. The manager asked her how long she has been designing logos. Sarah replied that she was working in marketing since 2022. She managed impressing the panel, so they promised calling her by the end of the week."


1. **Error:** `asked her how long she has been designing` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `she had been designing` (Reported speech rule: the present perfect "has been" must backshift to the past perfect "had been").
2. **Error:** `she was working... since 2022` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `she had been working...` (Because of the time marker "since 2022" in a past context/reported speech, the Past Perfect Continuous is required instead of Past Continuous).
3. **Error:** `managed impressing` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `managed to impress` (The verb "manage" must be followed by a to-infinitive, not a gerund).
4. **Error:** `promised calling` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `promised to call` (The verb "promise" also requires a to-infinitive).


### Passage 3: Overcoming Fears


> "When I was younger, I would be afraid of public speaking. My teacher told me that if I practiced in front of a mirror, I will feel more confident. I avoided to give presentations for years, but last week I finally delivered a speech without reading my notes."


1. **Error:** `I would be afraid` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I used to be afraid` (While "would" can describe past habits, it cannot be used with stative verbs like "be" to describe past states).
2. **Error:** `if I practiced... I will feel` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `I would feel` (This is reported/indirect speech of a conditional statement; "will" must backshift to "would").
3. **Error:** `avoided to give` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `avoided giving` (The verb "avoid" must be followed by a gerund, not a to-infinitive).

### Passage 1: A Missed Opportunity


> "If Alex would have studied harder last semester, he would be at university right now instead of retaking his exams. He feels like he wasted his time. By this time next year, however, he will graduate and can finally start his career. Little he knew that the final exam would be so intensely difficult."


1. **Error:** `If Alex would have studied harder` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `If Alex had studied harder` (Third/Mixed Conditional rule: the "if" clause takes the Past Perfect, never "would have").
2. **Error:** `he will graduate` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `he will have graduated` (The future time marker "By this time next year" requires the Future Perfect to show an action that will be completed by a certain point in the future).
3. **Error:** `Little he knew` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Little did he know` (Negative/restrictive adverbials like "Little" at the beginning of a sentence require subject-verb inversion).


### Passage 2: The Project Deadline


> "The team was exhausted because they had been working around the clock. The project should be finished by Friday, but a massive server crash delayed everything. Jessica must leave her laptop at the office yesterday because she didn't have it when she got home. Having realizing they were out of time, they asked for an extension."


1. **Error:** `The project should be finished by Friday` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `should have been finished` (To express an unfulfilled obligation or expectation in the past, you must use a past modal structure: *should + have + past participle*).
2. **Error:** `Jessica must leave her laptop` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `must have left her laptop` (For strong logical deductions about a *past* event, use *must have + past participle*).
3. **Error:** `Having realizing` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Having realized` (Perfect participle clauses require *Having + past participle* to show that one action happened before another).


### Passage 3: Urban Development


> "The city council is planning to build a new highway. Not only the project will cost millions of dollars, but it will also displace dozens of families. Many residents object against the plan, claiming that public transit should be prioritized. The mayor, whom has been under immense pressure, refused to comment on the matter."


1. **Error:** `Not only the project will cost` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Not only will the project cost` (When "Not only" begins a sentence, it triggers an inversion of the subject and the auxiliary verb).
2. **Error:** `object against the plan` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `object to the plan` (Incorrect dependent preposition; the correct collocation is always to *object to* something).
3. **Error:** `The mayor, whom has been` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `who has been` (The relative pronoun acts as the subject of the relative clause "has been under pressure," so the subject pronoun "who" must be used instead of the object pronoun "whom").

## B2.2:


### Passage 1: The Corporate Crisis


> "The CEO demanded that the marketing director resigns immediately following the PR disaster. Under no circumstances employees are allowed to speak to the press without prior clearance. Had the company responded sooner, they might not be facing such a severe backlash now. The board is currently considering to hire an external crisis management firm."


1. **Error:** `demanded that the marketing director resigns` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `resign` (The verb "demand" triggers the subjunctive mood in a *that*-clause, requiring the bare/base form of the verb, regardless of the subject).
2. **Error:** `Under no circumstances employees are allowed` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `Under no circumstances are employees allowed` (Negative adverbial phrases at the start of a sentence require subject-auxiliary inversion).
3. **Error:** `considering to hire` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `considering hiring` (The verb "consider" must be followed by a gerund, not a to-infinitive).
4. *Note:* `Had the company responded... they might not be facing...` is a correct **mixed conditional** (past action affecting present result), so it is not an error!


### Passage 2: Literary Criticisms


> "Hardly had the controversial novel been published when critics started tearing it apart. The author wishes she didn't write it so hastily, as the plot holes are glaringly obvious. I look forward to read her next book, though, because despite of her weak plotting, her prose style is absolutely mesmerizing. She really needs to have her work edit by a professional."


1. **Error:** `wishes she didn't write it` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `wishes she hadn't written it` (To express regret about a *past* action, "wish" must be followed by the Past Perfect, not the Past Simple).
2. **Error:** `look forward to read` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `look forward to reading` (In the phrase "look forward to," the word "to" acts as a preposition, which means it must be followed by a gerund).
3. **Error:** `despite of her weak plotting` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `despite her weak plotting` or `in spite of...` (The preposition "despite" is never followed by "of").
4. **Error:** `have her work edit` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `have her work edited` (The causative structure *have + object + past participle* is required here to show an action done by someone else).


### Passage 3: Eco-Tourism Debate


> "The small island has seen a massive influx of tourists, that has severely threatened the local ecosystem. The local government is accused of prioritizing profit over sustainability. It is high time they take drastic measures to limit visitors. Unless stricter regulations are implemented, irreversible damage will be done to the coral reefs, resulting in the island losing its primary appeal."


1. **Error:** `influx of tourists, that has...` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `, which has...` (In non-defining relative clauses—the ones separated by commas—you must use "which" instead of "that" to refer to the whole situation).
2. **Error:** `accused of prioritizing` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** *This structure is actually correct!* (Accuse + someone + of + -ing).
3. **Error:** `It is high time they take...` $\rightarrow$ **Correction:** `It is high time they took...` (The expression "It is high time" is a hypothetical structure that requires the Unreal Past / Past Simple to express urgency about a present action).
